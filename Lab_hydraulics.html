Welcome to the FIU Hydraulics Reseach Laboratory, Miami, FL

Overview

The FIU Hydraulics Reseach Laboratory (FHRL) is a new large-scale facility housed in the FIU Engineering Center, Miami, FL. This state-of the-art facility has a footprint of 4800 ft2 (446 m2) and features a ... recirculating system with the ability to test up to three simultaneous and independent experiments with flows up to 10.6 cfs (300 L/s). The facility consists of constant head water and air tanks, variable and constant speed pumps, and impulsion and return pipe lines.

A panoramic view of the FIU Outdoors Hydraulics Laboratory

Potential Test Cases

The facility can be used for testing most physical hydraulic models, including:

  • River hydraulic structures (open-channel flows)

  • High-head pressurized systems

  • Performance of various types of green infrastructure

  • Flood control

  • Air-water high-speed flows

  • Storm surge protection

  • Erosion and scour control

  • Stream restoration

Current and Previous projects


  • Long-term Performance of Various Types of Green Infrastructure to Reduce Land-Based Pollution in Biscayne Bay and Mitigate Floods and Urban Heat Island

  • A Physics-Based Artificial Intelligence General Framework for Optimal Control of Sewer Systems to Minimize Sewer Overflows

  • Violent geysers in stormsewer systems

  • Dynamic management of water storage in shallow and deep ponds

  • Remotely-operated system for water release of storage systems
